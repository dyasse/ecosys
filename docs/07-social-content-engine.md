# 7. Social Content Engine

## Platform Strategy Overview

| Platform | Primary Goal | Content Type | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pinterest | SEO + passive traffic | Static pins, idea pins | 3-5 pins/day (use scheduler) |
| Instagram | Brand awareness + trust | Reels + carousels | 4-5 posts/week |
| TikTok | Discovery + virality | Short videos (15-60 sec) | 3-4 videos/week |
| Facebook | Community + shares | Posts + blog links | 2-3 posts/week |

**Priority order for a beginner:** Pinterest first (passive, compounds over time), then Instagram, then TikTok.

---

## Tone Guide for All Platforms

**Use:** cozy, beautiful, easy, step-by-step, free, beginner-friendly, printable, instant download, handmade, elegant, create, make, stitch, cozy, warm, craft, pattern, tutorial

**Avoid:** SALE!!!, LIMITED TIME, BUY NOW, URGENT, cheap, deal, discount (unless genuinely offering one)

**The brand voice is:** A knowledgeable friend who makes beautiful things and loves to share. Not a marketer. Not a salesperson. A creator.

### Board Strategy

Create these main boards:
1. `Crochet Patterns for Beginners` — your patterns + curated pins
2. `Free Crochet Patterns` — specifically for lead magnets
3. `Crochet Gifts to Make` — gift-focused content
4. `Crochet Home Decor Patterns` — home decor category
5. `Knitting Patterns for Beginners` — if you sell knitting
6. `Printable Planners for Makers` — if you sell planners
7. `Crochet Tips and Tutorials` — blog posts + educational content

**Rule:** Always add your own pins to your own boards first, then add to group boards.

## TikTok / Reels Video Ideas

### 10 Video Concepts with Hooks

**Video 1 — The Reveal**
- Hook (first 3 seconds): Hold up a finished product and say: "I made this in one afternoon."
- Concept: Time-lapse of the making process, ending with the finished product. Show the PDF pattern on screen briefly.
- CTA: "Full pattern is at the link in my bio."

**Video 2 — The Problem/Solution**
- Hook: "Stop doing this with your crochet." (show the wrong way)
- Concept: Show one common mistake, then show the correct technique side by side.
- CTA: "Get my free beginner guide — link in bio."

**Video 3 — Stitch Breakdown**
- Hook: "I'm going to teach you this stitch in 30 seconds."
- Concept: Slow-motion tutorial of one specific stitch. Text overlay shows the steps.
- CTA: "Save this for later."

**Video 4 — Pattern Unboxing**
- Hook: "This is what my pattern PDF looks like inside."
- Concept: Screen recording or phone showing scrolling through the PDF. Highlight the photo tutorials, the stitch diagram, the supply list.
- CTA: "Download it at the link in my bio."

**Video 5 — "What I Made This Weekend"**
- Hook: Start mid-process: "I'm working on something..."
- Concept: Behind the scenes of your creative process. End with the finished product.
- CTA: "Pattern coming soon — follow so you don't miss it."

**Video 6 — Beginner Tips**
- Hook: "Three things I wish I knew before I started crocheting."
- Concept: Talking-head style, share 3 genuine tips with visual demonstrations.
- CTA: "More tips in my free guide — link in bio."

**Video 7 — "This is Why I Do This"**
- Hook: Show a beautiful photo/video of a finished product.
- Concept: Personal brand story video — why you started making patterns, who you make them for.
- CTA: "Join my community — link in bio."

**Video 8 — Yarn Haul / Review**
- Hook: "I tested 5 different yarns for this project."
- Concept: Show each yarn, rate it for [product type], recommend your favorite.
- CTA: "The pattern I used is in my bio."

**Video 9 — Gift Idea Video**
- Hook: "Looking for a handmade gift idea? Make this."
- Concept: Show finished product styled as a gift. Text overlay: "Beginner-friendly · 1 weekend · Free pattern."
- CTA: "Free pattern + more at the link in bio."

**Video 10 — "Things I Don't Say in My Patterns"**
- Hook: "Here are the secrets crochet patterns never tell you."
- Concept: Share 3-5 unwritten rules of crochet that beginners don't know.
- CTA: "Get my beginner's guide — link in bio."

## Facebook Content

### 5 Facebook Post Ideas

**Post 1 — Educational Share**
Share your blog post with a genuine introduction:
"I wrote this for everyone who's ever felt confused reading a crochet pattern. The abbreviations alone can make you feel like giving up — but once you learn the system, they make complete sense. I broke it all down in this post: [link]"

**Post 2 — Community Question**
"What's on your hooks this week? Drop a photo or description below — I love seeing what everyone's making."

**Post 3 — Behind the Scenes**
Share a photo of your workspace or work in progress with a genuine caption about your process.

**Post 4 — Free Gift Share**
"I made something for you. A free guide to reading crochet patterns — perfect if you're just starting out or if patterns still confuse you. No sign-up needed for the blog post, or grab the printable version: [lead magnet link]"

**Post 5 — Soft Product Share**
"I finished the [Product Name] pattern this week and I'm really proud of how it came out. If you're looking for your next project, this might be it: [product page link] — and if you're not ready to buy, the blog has lots of free tips and tutorials too."
