# 8. SEO Engine — Blog Strategy

## SEO Fundamentals for Your Business

**How this works:**
1. You write a blog post targeting a specific search query
2. Google indexes the post
3. People searching for that query find your post
4. Your post links to your lead magnet or product
5. Visitors convert to subscribers or buyers

**Timeline:** SEO takes 3-6 months to show results. Start now. Every post compounds.

**Tools needed:**
- Google Search Console (free) — see what queries bring traffic
- Google Analytics 4 (free) — see what users do on your site
- Ubersuggest free tier OR Ahrefs free tools — keyword research
- Your brain + Claude — content creation

## Blog Post Structure (Use for Every Post)

```
TITLE (H1) — includes primary keyword
├── INTRODUCTION (150 words)
│   ├── Hook: relatable problem or question
│   ├── Bridge: "In this post, you'll learn..."
│   └── Keyword usage: include primary keyword naturally
│
├── SECTION 1 (H2) — foundational answer (250 words)
├── SECTION 2 (H2) — deeper dive or step-by-step (250 words)
├── SECTION 3 (H2) — common questions or mistakes (250 words)
├── SECTION 4 (H2) — related tips or resources (250 words)
│   └── [NATURAL CTA: mention lead magnet or related product]
│
└── CONCLUSION (100 words)
    ├── Summarize key point
    ├── CTA: "Get the free [lead magnet name] →"
    └── Keyword in conclusion naturally
```

**Target length:** 1,200–1,800 words  
**Target keyword density:** 1-2% (don't stuff)  
**Images:** Minimum 2 per post (your own photos or Canva graphics)

## Full Blog Post Outline Example

### Post: "How to Read a Crochet Pattern: Complete Beginner's Guide"

**Primary keyword:** how to read a crochet pattern  
**Secondary keywords:** crochet abbreviations, crochet pattern for beginners, crochet pattern symbols  
**Search volume:** 8,100/month (US)  
**Difficulty:** Low-Medium  
**Intent:** Informational — person wants to learn

**Outline:**

```
H1: How to Read a Crochet Pattern: Complete Beginner's Guide

INTRODUCTION (150 words)
- Hook: "The first time I picked up a crochet pattern, I was convinced I needed 
  a decoder ring. 'sc 2, ch 3, dc in next st' — what?"
- Bridge: "In this guide, I'll walk you through exactly how to read any crochet 
  pattern, from the supply list to the finishing instructions."
- Include "how to read a crochet pattern" in paragraph 1

H2: What's In a Crochet Pattern? (The Anatomy)
- Materials section (yarn, hook, extras)
- Gauge section
- Special stitches
- Pattern notes
- Row-by-row instructions

H2: Crochet Abbreviations Explained (The Most Common Ones)
- Create a table: Abbreviation | Full Name | Description
- ch, sc, dc, hdc, tr, sl st, sk, sp, rep, yo, etc.
- Mention US vs UK differences

H2: How to Follow Crochet Instructions Step by Step
- Read the whole pattern once before starting
- Gather all materials first
- Make a gauge swatch
- How to count stitches
- What to do when you get confused

H2: Common Mistakes When Reading a Crochet Pattern
- Skipping the gauge
- Confusing US and UK terms
- Not reading pattern notes
- Losing your place (tip: use a row counter)

CONCLUSION (100 words)
- "Reading crochet patterns gets easier every time — I promise."
- SOFT CTA: "If you want a printable reference guide to keep beside you 
  while you crochet, I made one for you. It's free — grab it below."
- [Download the Free Pattern Guide →] button
```

**Internal links in this post:**
- Link to "Crochet Abbreviations: Complete Guide" (post #3)
- Link to product: "If you're ready for your first project, try [Product Name]"
- Link to lead magnet page: /free-gift

## Internal Linking Strategy

**Rule:** Every blog post must link to at least:
1. One other blog post (related content)
2. One lead magnet page OR one product page

**Anchor text examples:**
- "beginner crochet patterns" → links to your product page
- "free crochet guide" → links to /free-gift
- "how to choose yarn" → links to the yarn guide blog post

**Never use:** "Click here" or "Read more" as anchor text. Always use descriptive anchor text with keywords.

## CTA Placement in Blog Posts

**Rule:** Every post needs 3 CTA opportunities:

| Position | CTA Type | Example |
|---|---|---|
| After introduction | Soft mention | "P.S. If you want the printable version of this guide, grab it free →" |
| Mid-post (after section 2) | Value-based | "I made a free cheat sheet for exactly this — [download it here]" |
| End of post | Direct CTA | Button: "Get the Free [Lead Magnet Name] →" |

## SEO Technical Checklist (For Each Post)

- [ ] Primary keyword in H1 (page title)
- [ ] Primary keyword in first 100 words
- [ ] Primary keyword in at least one H2
- [ ] Primary keyword in meta description
- [ ] Meta description under 155 characters
- [ ] URL slug includes keyword (e.g., `/blog/how-to-read-a-crochet-pattern`)
- [ ] At least 2 internal links
- [ ] At least 1 external link (to credible source like a stitch dictionary)
- [ ] Images have alt text with descriptive keywords
- [ ] Post is over 1,000 words
- [ ] Published with correct date
- [ ] Submitted to Google Search Console after publishing
