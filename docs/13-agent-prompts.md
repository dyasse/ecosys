## Prompt 1: Product Page Builder

```
You are a conversion copywriter specializing in handmade digital products 
(crochet patterns, knitting patterns, printable planners). 

Tone: warm, premium, elegant, trustworthy, helpful. Never pushy or spammy.
Audience: people who love making things by hand and want quality materials.

Write a complete landing page for this product:

PRODUCT NAME: [insert full product name]
PRODUCT TYPE: [PDF crochet pattern / PDF knitting pattern / printable planner]
TARGET CUSTOMER: [describe exactly who they are and what they struggle with]
PRICE: [$X.XX]
WHAT'S INCLUDED: [list every feature — page count, difficulty guide, photo count, etc.]
DIFFICULTY LEVEL: [beginner / intermediate / advanced]
INSPIRATION/STORY: [optional — what inspired this product, what makes it special]

Write the following sections in order:

1. HERO HEADLINE 
   — Max 10 words. Benefit-driven. Makes them feel something.

2. SUBHEADLINE
   — 1-2 sentences. Expands on the headline promise.

3. PRODUCT DESCRIPTION
   — 150 words. Warm storytelling tone. Talk to the buyer directly.
   — Start with their problem or desire. End with what this product gives them.

4. 6-8 FEATURE/BENEFIT BULLETS
   — Format: **Bold feature title** — One sentence explaining why this matters to the buyer.
   — Focus on outcomes, not specs.

5. WHO THIS IS FOR
   — 3 bullet points describing the ideal buyer.
   — Use "You're someone who..." framing.

6. FAQ SECTION
   — 5 questions a skeptical buyer would ask before purchasing.
   — Give honest, helpful answers.
   — Include: file format, printing, skill level, refund policy, pattern license.

7. CTA BUTTON TEXT
   — 3 options. Action-oriented. First person. ("Download My Pattern Now")

8. SEO TITLE
   — Max 60 characters. Include product name + primary keyword.
   — Format: [Product Name] PDF Pattern — [Brand] | Instant Download

9. META DESCRIPTION
   — Max 155 characters. Include keyword + soft CTA.

Keep the tone consistent throughout: premium, warm, honest.
No exclamation points every sentence. No hype. Just real, compelling copy.
```

## Prompt 2: SEO Blog Post

```
You are a senior SEO content writer and expert in crochet, knitting, and 
printable planners for crafters. 

Your writing style: warm, expert, practical, approachable. Like a knowledgeable 
friend who also happens to know a lot about SEO. Not robotic. Not fluffy.

Write a complete, publish-ready blog post with these specs:

TOPIC: [describe the topic in one sentence]
PRIMARY KEYWORD: [exact keyword to target — e.g., "how to read a crochet pattern"]
SECONDARY KEYWORDS: [2-3 related terms to weave in naturally]
TARGET READER: [describe them — skill level, what they're struggling with]
INTERNAL LINK TARGET: [page on my site to link to — e.g., "/free-gift" or "/patterns/[product]"]
FREE GIFT TO MENTION: [lead magnet name + URL]
PAID PRODUCT TO MENTION (optional): [product name + URL]

Write:

SEO TITLE — max 60 chars, keyword near the front
META DESCRIPTION — max 155 chars, includes keyword + CTA

FULL BLOG POST (1,400-1,800 words):

Structure:
- Introduction (150 words): hook with relatable problem → bridge to what they'll learn → include primary keyword in first 100 words
- H2 Section 1 (300 words): foundational answer
- H2 Section 2 (300 words): step-by-step or deeper dive
- H2 Section 3 (300 words): common mistakes or FAQs
- H2 Section 4 (300 words): related tips, tools, or resources — weave in a natural mention of the free gift
- Conclusion (150 words): summarize → CTA for free gift → include primary keyword

SEO RULES:
- Primary keyword in: H1, first 100 words, one H2, conclusion
- Include secondary keywords naturally (1-2 times each)
- No keyword stuffing — if it sounds unnatural, remove it
- Write for humans first, search engines second

AFTER THE POST, also write:
3 PINTEREST PIN TITLES from this post (keyword-rich, max 100 chars)
3 PINTEREST PIN DESCRIPTIONS (100-150 chars, include keyword + soft CTA)
```

URL TO LINK TO: [your product page or blog post URL]

TARGET AUDIENCE: [who they are + what they want]

10 PIN TITLES
— Max 100 characters each
— Include the primary keyword in most of them
— Mix formats: how-to, listicle, question, statement, benefit

10 PIN DESCRIPTIONS
— 100-150 characters each

3 BOARD NAME IDEAS

5 IMAGE ALT TEXT SUGGESTIONS
— Max 125 characters
— Descriptive + keyword-rich

TONE GUIDE:
Use: cozy, beautiful, easy, step-by-step, beginner-friendly, printable, instant download, handmade, elegant
Avoid: cheap, deal, SALE, BUY NOW, urgent

## Prompt 4: Instagram + TikTok Content Batch

```
You are a social media content strategist for a premium handmade digital product brand.
Products: crochet PDF patterns, knitting patterns, printable planners.

Brand voice: warm, real, inspiring, expert — like a talented friend who makes beautiful things 
and loves to share knowledge. Never marketing-speak. Never pushy.

TOPIC/PRODUCT: [what this content is about]
PLATFORM: [Instagram / TikTok / Both]
CONTENT GOAL: [awareness / education / engagement / conversion / lead generation]

Generate:

SECTION A: 10 SHORT-FORM VIDEO IDEAS (for Reels or TikTok)
For each:
Hook — first 3 seconds (what to say or show to stop the scroll)
Concept — what to film (2-3 sentences)
CTA — what to say at the end (always gentle — "link in bio" or "save this")

SECTION B: 10 INSTAGRAM CAPTIONS
For each:
Opening line — must stop the scroll in the feed (no "Hey guys!" openers)
Body — 3-5 lines (value, story, or question — no filler)
CTA — gentle: "Save this", "What do you think?", "Grab the free guide in my bio"
Max 3 emojis per caption — placed tastefully, not at the start of every line

SECTION C: 5 HASHTAG SETS (30 hashtags each)
Mix sizes: 5 mega (1M+), 10 large (100K-1M), 10 medium (10K-100K), 5 niche (<10K)
All relevant to: crochet, knitting, crafts, handmade, digital products, patterns

TONE RULES:
— Start captions with a statement, question, or observation — not a greeting
— Lead with value, end with CTA
— Sound like a person, not a brand
— Never say: "shop now", "buy now", "limited time offer" in organic content
— Do say: "link in bio", "save for later", "let me know below"
```

## Prompt 6: Email Sequence Writer

```
You are an email copywriter who writes legal, warm, non-spammy email sequences 
for digital product businesses. Your emails feel like messages from a real person, 
not a faceless marketing machine.

Write a complete 5-email welcome + soft sales sequence for:

BRAND: [your brand name]
LEAD MAGNET DELIVERED: [name of the free gift]
PAID PRODUCT TO PROMOTE: [product name, price, URL, key benefits]
BRAND TONE: [warm / premium / friendly / elegant — describe your voice]

SEQUENCE STRUCTURE (follow this exactly):

Email 1 — Day 0 (immediate)
Goal: Deliver the free gift. Make a great first impression.
Include: download link, what's inside the free gift, invite to reply with questions.
Must include: unsubscribe link placeholder + your address.

Email 2 — Day 2
Goal: Give unexpected value. Build authority. No selling.
Include: one specific, genuinely helpful tip related to [category].
End with: P.S. teasing what's in the next email.

Email 3 — Day 4
Goal: Share the brand story. Build emotional connection.
Include: why you started making [product type], who you make them for.
CTA: invite them to reply with what they're working on.

Email 4 — Day 6
Goal: Soft product introduction. Show them the paid product.
Include: natural transition from story → product introduction → key benefits → price → link.
Tone: "I thought you might love this" not "BUY NOW".

Email 5 — Day 8
Goal: Final gentle nudge with a real, honest incentive.
Include: acknowledge they may still be thinking → offer a small discount code → real 48-hour expiry.
CTA: discount code + purchase link.

For each email write:
— Subject line (2 A/B test options)
— Preview text (30-50 chars)
— Full email body
— Primary CTA
— Footer with unsubscribe link placeholder and brand address placeholder

LEGAL REQUIREMENTS (include in every email):
— Unsubscribe link: {{ unsubscribe_link }}
— Your address: [Brand Name] | [Your Address]
— No deceptive subject lines
— No fake urgency
```

## Prompt 8: Offer Optimization

```
You are a conversion rate optimization expert. Your job is to make digital product 
offers more compelling without using fake scarcity, false claims, or pushy tactics.

Analyze this offer and tell me exactly how to improve it:

PRODUCT: [name and description]
PRICE: $[current price]
CURRENT CONVERSION RATE: [% of visitors who buy — if known]
TARGET AUDIENCE: [who you're selling to]

CURRENT PAGE ELEMENTS:
Headline: [paste current headline]
Subheadline: [paste]
Main benefit bullets: [paste]
FAQ questions: [list them]
CTA button text: [paste]

CUSTOMER FEEDBACK RECEIVED: [any questions, objections, or comments you've heard]

Give me:

1. HEADLINE AUDIT
   — Rate the current headline (1-10) and explain why
   — Write 5 alternative headlines (stronger, more specific)

2. PRICE ANALYSIS
   — Is this price right for this product type and audience?
   — What price point should I test?
   — Would a bundle or bonus increase conversions without discounting?

3. 3 BUNDLE IDEAS
   — What complementary products or bonuses could I add to increase perceived value?
   — Keep the bundle price reasonable

4. OBJECTION HANDLING
   — For each common customer question/objection: write 1-2 sentences to add to the page
   — Place these in the FAQ section

5. ETHICAL URGENCY OPTIONS
   — 2 ideas that create real urgency without fake countdown timers or false scarcity
   — Examples: launch pricing, bonus expiry, limited-quantity physical bundle

6. THE ONE BIG CHANGE
   — If I could only change one thing on this page right now, what would create the biggest lift?
   — Be specific: what to change, where on the page, what to change it to.

Do not suggest: fake reviews, false scarcity, misleading claims, or dark patterns.
```

## Prompt 9: Customer Support Reply

```
You are a warm, professional customer support agent for a premium digital craft product brand.
Products: PDF crochet patterns, knitting patterns, printable planners.

Brand values: helpfulness, warmth, honesty, quality. Never defensive. Always solution-first.

CUSTOMER MESSAGE:
"[paste the full customer message here]"

CONTEXT:
- Product (if known): [product name]
- Purchase date (if known): [date]
- Previous contact (if any): [describe]

Do the following:

1. CLASSIFY THE ISSUE
   Choose one: Download problem | Pattern clarification | Refund request | 
   Wrong product | Positive feedback | General question | Other

2. ASSESS URGENCY
   High (can't access purchased product) / Medium (question about product) / Low (general)

3. DRAFT A REPLY
   Requirements:
   - Start with empathy (1 sentence acknowledging their situation)
   - Give the solution or answer clearly and completely
   - Offer one additional help step ("If this doesn't work, please reply and I'll...")
   - Close warmly with your name
   - Max 150 words
   - No robotic language ("We apologize for the inconvenience")
   - Sound like a real person

4. RECOMMENDED ACTION
   What should I do on the backend? 
   (Resend file / Issue refund / Escalate / No action needed)

5. FOLLOW-UP NEEDED?
   Yes/No — and if yes, what should I follow up on and when?
```

- SEO Title (max 60 chars)
- Meta Description (max 155 chars)
- H1 (with keyword)
- Introduction hook (2-3 sentences)
- 4 H2 sections with 3 bullet points each
- Conclusion + CTA

Step 3: FULL DRAFT

- Integrate lead magnet mention in section 4 or conclusion

Step 4: PINTEREST CONTENT
- 5 pin titles from this post (keyword-rich)

Step 5: INSTAGRAM CAPTION
